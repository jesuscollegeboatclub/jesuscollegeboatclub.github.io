The race

A Cambridge institution

The Fairbairn Cup is a head race organised by Jesus College Boat Club and raced on the Cam in the ninth week of the Michaelmas term. There are divisions for both novice and senior crews, with the 2025 races taking place at the beginning of December over two consecutive days — novices first, then seniors.

The entry form is published on this website each autumn. For the complete set of rules, please consult the Rules and Regulations. All coxes and bank parties of novice crews must be familiar with the Coxes' Notes.

Eligibility

Who can race

The races are open to all Cambridge and Oxford colleges. Non-college clubs and ineligible college crews (such as alumni crews) may race in Invitational divisions, with prizes awarded separately.

Only members of college (in statu pupillare) may row in a college boat, and crews race within their respective divisions. A 'Novice' crew is one in which no rower has rowed, and no cox has coxed, prior to October of the current year.

Trialists: college 1st VIII colours may not row below their 2nd VIII, and current CUBC trialists (and Oxford equivalents) may not row in college boats.

On the day

Marshalling & safety

The river closes for the races from 08:30 until lighting-up on both days. No on-water warm-up is permitted after 08:30 — all crews warm up on land before their division.

The first 10 crews must be in the Start Marshalling Area at least 30 minutes before the published start; all others 20 minutes before. Latecomers causing delay receive a 5-second penalty.

For safety, there is no overtaking before the Men's Top Finish. Crews must keep rowing across the line and follow marshals' instructions — any crew that fails to do so at the finish is automatically disqualified.
